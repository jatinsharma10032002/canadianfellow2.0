Hourly Rentals

Choose the Right Vehicle

  • Information: Gather information about the available vehicle types, sizes, and features. Consider factors like the number of passengers, luggage space, and fuel efficiency. Check the rental agency's website or contact them for details.

  • Requirements: To rent a vehicle, you typically need to be of a certain age (usually 21 or 25 years old), possess a valid driver's license, and provide payment information. Some rentals may have specific vehicle type requirements based on the driver's license class.

  • Procedure: Browse the rental agency's vehicle options online or in-person at their location. Once you've chosen the right vehicle, provide your personal details, driver's license, and payment information to complete the reservation.

Decide Rental Duration

  • Information: Understand your trip's duration and how long you'll need the vehicle. Rental agencies offer various rental durations, including hourly, daily, weekly, and monthly options, each with different pricing structures.

  • Requirements: There are usually no specific requirements related to rental duration. However, for longer rentals, you may need to provide additional personal and payment information.

  • Procedure: During the reservation process, specify the start and end times of your rental duration. Ensure that your rental aligns with your travel plans, and confirm the associated costs.

Book in Advance

  • Information: Know that rental availability can vary based on location and demand. Booking in advance can secure your preferred vehicle type and rental period, especially during peak seasons or at busy rental locations.

  • Requirements: Advance booking typically requires providing your personal details, driver's license information, and a valid credit card for the reservation deposit.

  • Procedure: Visit the rental agency's website or call their reservation center to book your vehicle in advance. Follow the prompts to enter your information and secure your reservation with a deposit.
